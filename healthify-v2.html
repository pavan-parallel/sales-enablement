Chapter 02

The diagnosis.

Twenty interviews with the people who stayed and the people who left.

Phase 1 of 3 · Research→a precise diagnosis

The diagnosis

We started with twenty people.

Engineered on five axes — operating system · new vs existing · profession · geography · AI usage A product manager in Delhi on iOS behaves nothing like a small-business owner in Coimbatore on Android — the insights live in the differences. 20 sessions, 60–75 minutes each.

The turn

Chapter 03

The exploration.

Twelve AI concepts, put in front of the people who would live with them.

Phase 2 of 3 · Design & Validate→a validated build roadmap

The exploration

We designed twelve AI concepts.

Then users passed judgement.

So the verdicts rewrote the roadmap.

Tested with a fresh participant mix — existing all-active users, next-gen users, and new users, from metros to small towns — to separate genuine signal from novelty.

Personalised meal planning

The clear winner — 'Picked for you' was the most popular concept in the study, 11 of 13 keen.

Multi-modal & assistive

Users are loyal to one modality, chosen for accuracy. Every new modality must earn its trust.

Community & social

Peers' preferences, celebrity diets and polls failed to resonate. Curiosity, not utility.

Personal context won. External signals didn't.

The ship

Tracking became the one obvious action again.

Trust, by design
  • For users to trust AI they have to understand it — so the AI's reasoning is visible and easy to grasp.
