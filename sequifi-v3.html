Chapter 01 · The setup

Sequifi makes HR and payroll software for the home-services industry. Roofing, pest control, solar, lawn care.
The industry sells door to door. Its crews are young, hired in batches, and paid on commission.
That pay is the most complicated part of the product. And it belongs to users with no patience for enterprise software.
Sequifi had found its market. The next stage was getting the HR that still ran on spreadsheets into the product, and they decided design was how.

Chapter 02 · The pay problem

Hiring one rep means building their whole pay plan. Wages, commission, upfront bonuses, overrides, milestones.
Every product carries its own rates. Solar pays differently from pest control, and managers earn a share of every sale under them.
The rates don't sit still either. Every change needs a record of what moved, when it takes effect, and who approved it.

Chapter 04 · Onboarding

Onboarding a worker took eight steps, and the three pay steps repeated for every product a rep sold.
The audit found worse. You couldn't move between steps, errors never said where they were, and leaving the flow meant walking through all of it again.
We rebuilt the flow around the product. Solar's commission, upfront and overrides now sit together on one page, set up once. Eight steps became six.
We also made it forgiving. Drafts save, steps can wait, and an error points at the exact field it comes from.
Workers got the same treatment. Their own onboarding went from seven steps to five, and offer letters moved into SequiDocs, read and signed from a phone.

Chapter 05 · The design system

The product had grown for years without a design language. Every screen solved its own problems its own way.
The new language leans on two ideas. Order, because this software moves paychecks. Warmth, because half its users are on their first job.
We took two complete directions to Sequifi. They chose Opscape: calm, functional, built for operations.
Then we built the system itself. Haffer for type, a restrained palette, and every component in every state a screen can reach: loading, empty, error, disabled.
Every component ships with its manual: when to use it, when not to, what to reach for instead. Sequifi's team can put up a new screen without calling us.

Chapter 06 · The turn

We delivered all three. The system, the new onboarding, and the module that proved the language on the real product.
Then the brief changed. Halfway through the engagement, Sequifi asked us to redesign the entire platform.
the design system

Chapter 07 · The rebuild

Fifteen modules, one language.
The pay plan from chapter two became a guided wizard. Wages through settlements, one product at a time.
Rate changes leave a trail now. The amount, the date it takes effect, and the person who made it.
Some data reads better as a picture. Positions double as an org chart, locations as a map.
The rest of the platform followed. Payroll, hiring, reports, automations, permissions, documents.
The rebuild tested the system as much as the product. Where a real screen broke a component, we fixed the system, so it stayed fixed everywhere.
