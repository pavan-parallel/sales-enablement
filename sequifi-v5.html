Chapter 01 · The setup

Sequifi makes HR and payroll software for home-services companies. Roofing, pest control, solar, lawn care.
These companies hire door-to-door sales crews. People join in batches, and most don't stay long.
Every rep is paid on commission. So every hire means building a pay plan from scratch. That work lands on HR.
Sequifi had the customers. The problem was usage: HR still did most of this work in spreadsheets. To grow, the product had to be easier than the spreadsheet.

Chapter 02 · The problem

A rep's pay plan has five parts, set up in order. Wages, commission, upfront bonus, overrides, milestones.
Commission isn't one number. Every product has its own rate. The same goes for upfronts and overrides.
And the product itself had aged. Years of new features, with no design language holding them together.
So Sequifi came to Parallel with three asks. Make onboarding simple. Build a design system. Redesign one module to show the way.

Chapter 03 · Onboarding

Onboarding one worker took eight steps.
Three of those steps were about pay. Each one listed every product the rep could sell.
Which meant one product's pay lived in three different places. Nobody could see it in one go.
The audit turned up more. Small things, but HR hit them every single day.
We regrouped the flow by product. One page now holds everything a product pays. Eight steps became six.
And we made it forgiving. Drafts save. Steps can wait. Errors point at the exact field.

Chapter 04 · The design system

The design system had a clear brief, in Sequifi's own words. Familiar, trustworthy, organized, clean.
We built two complete directions. Sequifi chose Opscape: clear, functional, made for operations.
Then the system itself. Hanken Grotesk for type, a restrained palette, and every component in every state a screen can reach.
